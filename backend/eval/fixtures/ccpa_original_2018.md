# CCPA — Original 2018 Text (Before the CPRA Amendments)

Source: California Consumer Privacy Act of 2018, Assembly Bill No. 375, original Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.140(c)(1) as enacted in 2018 (before the CPRA amendments that took effect January 1, 2023).

## Definition of a covered "business" (2018)

As originally enacted, the CCPA defined a "business" subject to the law as a for-profit entity doing business in California that collects consumers' personal information and satisfies at least one of the following thresholds:

- has annual gross revenues in excess of 25 million US dollars;
- alone or in combination, annually buys, receives for the business's commercial purposes, sells, or shares for commercial purposes the personal information of 50,000 or more consumers, households, or devices; or
- derives 50 percent or more of its annual revenues from selling consumers' personal information.

Under the original 2018 text, the second threshold was triggered at 50,000 consumers, households, or devices, and devices were explicitly counted toward that threshold.

## Scope of consumer rights (2018)

The original 2018 act gave consumers the right to know what personal information was collected, the right to delete personal information, the right to opt out of the sale of personal information, and the right to non-discrimination. The original act addressed the "sale" of personal information; it did not yet include the separate concept of "sharing" for cross-context behavioral advertising, which was added later by the CPRA.
