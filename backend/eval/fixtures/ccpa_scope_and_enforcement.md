# CCPA — Scope and Enforcement

Source: California Consumer Privacy Act of 2018 (CCPA), as amended by the CPRA.
Statutory thresholds and penalties from Cal. Civ. Code §§ 1798.140, 1798.150 and 1798.155.

## Who the CCPA applies to

The CCPA applies to for-profit businesses that do business in California, collect California consumers' personal information, and meet at least one of the following thresholds:

- have annual gross revenue of more than 25 million US dollars;
- buy, sell, or share the personal information of 100,000 or more California consumers or households; or
- derive 50 percent or more of their annual revenue from selling or sharing California consumers' personal information.

Businesses below all of these thresholds are generally not subject to the CCPA. Non-profit organizations and government agencies are also outside its scope.

## Civil penalties

Businesses that violate the CCPA can be subject to civil penalties of up to 2,500 US dollars for each unintentional violation and up to 7,500 US dollars for each intentional violation or each violation involving the personal information of consumers under 16 years of age. Enforcement is carried out by the California Privacy Protection Agency and the California Attorney General.

## Data breaches and the private right of action

Unlike the GDPR, the CCPA does not require businesses to notify a supervisory authority within a fixed deadline. Instead, the CCPA gives consumers a private right of action when their non-encrypted and non-redacted personal information is subject to unauthorized access and exfiltration, theft, or disclosure as a result of the business's failure to maintain reasonable security. In such cases consumers may recover statutory damages of between 100 and 750 US dollars per consumer per incident, or actual damages, whichever is greater.
